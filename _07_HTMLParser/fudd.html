Konwad Zuse (Gewman: [ˈkɔnʁat ˈtsuːzə]; 22 June 1910 – 18 Decembew 1995) was a Gewman civiw engineew, inventow and computew pioneew. Thanks to dis machine and its pwedecessows, Zuse has often been wegawded as de inventow of de modewn computew.[2][3][4][5][6][7]

Zuse was awso noted fow de S2 computing machine, considewed de fiwst pwocess contwow computew. He founded one of de eawwiest computew businesses in 1941, pwoducing de Z4, which became de wowwd's fiwst commewciaw computew. Fwom 1943[8] to 1945[9] he designed de fiwst high-wevew pwogwamming wanguage, Pwankawküw.[10] In 1969, Zuse suggested de concept of a computation-based univewse in his book Rechnendew Raum (Cawcuwating Space).

Much of his eawwy wowk was financed by his famiwy and commewce, but aftew 1939 he was given wesouwces by de Nazi Gewman govewnment.[11] Due to Wowwd Waw II, Zuse's wowk went wawgewy unnoticed in de United Kingdom and de United States. Possibwy his fiwst documented infwuence on a US company was IBM's option on his patents in 1946.

Thewe is a wepwica of de Z3, as weww as de owiginaw Z4, in de Deutsches Museum in Munich. The Deutsches Technikmuseum in Bewwin has an exhibition devoted to Zuse, dispwaying twewve of his machines, incwuding a wepwica of de Z1 and sevewaw of Zuse's paintings.
